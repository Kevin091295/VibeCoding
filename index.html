Born in Thiruvananthapuram on 11 November 1994, Sanju Samson grew up absorbing cricket in Delhi before his father moved the family to Kerala — a move that shaped a cricketing destiny.

With hands quick as reflex, forearms like steel, and eyes calibrated to the split-second, Samson represents rare elegance in a power-hitting era. He doesn't just bat — he composes.

The Journey

Career
Milestones

2011
First-Class Debut for Kerala
At just 17, Samson announced himself on the Ranji circuit — the youngest to captain a Kerala Ranji side.
2013
IPL Debut • Rajasthan Royals
Signed at 18, won the Best Young Player award with 206 runs and 13 catches. A star was born.
2015
India T20I Debut vs Zimbabwe
Made his international debut, beginning a long journey of intermittent appearances before cementing his spot.
2019
Unbeaten 212 in Vijay Hazare Trophy
The then-highest individual List-A score by an Indian wicketkeeper. Fastest double century in the format. A record-breaking masterpiece.
2021
Named Captain of Rajasthan Royals
Scored a century on captaincy debut vs PBKS — the first captain to do so in IPL history.
2022
Led RR to IPL Final
Rajasthan's best IPL campaign in years, reaching the final before falling to Gujarat Titans.
2024
Historic T20I Triple Century Year
First batter to score 3 T20I centuries in a single calendar year. Part of India's T20 World Cup winning squad.
2025–26
Traded to Chennai Super Kings
In a high-profile trade involving Jadeja & Curran, Samson joins CSK. Scored 97* in the 2026 T20 World Cup vs West Indies.
